The Johnson farm project involved installing a complete pattern tile system across 320 acres of heavy clay soil in Cass County. The field had chronic drainage issues with 45 acres of standing water every spring that prevented timely planting.

## The Challenge

This quarter section had been problematic for over a decade. The heavy clay soils in this area of Cass County hold water well into late spring, often preventing planting until June when conditions finally dried out. The landowner was losing significant yield potential and dealing with uneven crop emergence.

## Our Solution

We designed a systematic pattern tile layout with 4-inch laterals spaced at 40-foot intervals, perfectly suited for the clay soil type. Main lines were installed at strategic collection points with proper grade to ensure efficient water removal to the outlet.

The GPS-guided installation ensured precise depth control across the rolling terrain, maintaining optimal grade for consistent drainage performance.

## Results

The transformation was immediate. Spring 2023 saw the entire quarter planted on schedule in April, compared to the previous pattern of June planting on the wet areas. The landowner reported a 28% yield increase on the previously problematic acres.
