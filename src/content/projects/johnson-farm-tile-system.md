The Johnson Farm project involved installing a comprehensive drainage system across 320 acres of productive farmland in Cass County. The property had significant water management challenges, with 45 acres regularly flooded during spring months, delaying planting and reducing yields across the entire quarter section.

## Project Details

Our team conducted a detailed topographic survey using RTK GPS to map the field's natural drainage patterns and identify optimal tile placement. The design included:

- **Pattern Tile:** 4" corrugated pipe installed at 50-foot spacing
- **Main Lines:** 8" and 6" collector lines to manage high water volume
- **Outlets:** Two engineered outlet structures with erosion control
- **Total Length:** 8.5 miles of tile installed

## Installation Process

The installation was completed over 8 working days using our GPS-guided tile plow. Weather cooperated throughout the project, allowing us to maintain consistent depth and grade across the varying soil conditions.

Key installation highlights:
- Precise depth control maintained at 3.5-4 feet
- Minimal surface disruption to growing crops
- All outlet structures built to county specifications
- Complete restoration of field surfaces upon completion

## Results

The results have exceeded expectations:
- **22% average yield increase** across the drained area
- **45 acres of previously unusable land** now fully productive
- **Earlier planting** by 10-14 days in wet springs
- **Uniform crop emergence** across the entire field

The system has performed flawlessly through two growing seasons, including the exceptionally wet spring of 2024.
