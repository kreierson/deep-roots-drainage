This Richland County project demonstrates how targeted tile placement can be extremely cost-effective when properly planned. Rather than pattern tiling the entire quarter, we focused on natural collection areas and chronic wet spots.

## The Approach

After conducting a detailed topographic survey, we identified the key areas where water naturally collected during wet periods. By installing strategic main lines and targeted laterals in these zones, we could address 90% of the drainage issues with about 60% of the tile footage required for a full pattern system.

## Smart Design

The system captures water from the higher areas and efficiently moves it through the problem zones to the outlet. This approach allowed the farm to maximize their drainage investment while staying within budget.

## Excellent Results

The targeted approach delivered a 22% yield increase on the affected acres while keeping installation costs reasonable. The farm plans to add additional pattern tile in future years as budget allows.
